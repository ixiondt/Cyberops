## Role Purpose
The Network Analyst analyzes network behavior and communications to detect, assess, and report anomalous or malicious activity in support of defensive and offensive cyberspace operations.

This role focuses on **traffic patterns, protocols, and network architecture**, not host internals or operational planning.

---

## Core Skill Domains

### 1. Network Traffic Analysis
- Understand network communication patterns and flows
- Identify abnormal or suspicious traffic behavior
- Distinguish baseline traffic from anomalous activity

### 2. Protocol & Service Awareness
- Understand common network protocols and services
- Identify misuse or abuse of standard protocols
- Recognize unexpected protocol behavior

### 3. Network Architecture & Topology
- Understand segmentation, boundaries, and choke points
- Relate traffic observations to network design
- Identify high-risk paths and critical network nodes

### 4. Anomaly & Pattern Detection
- Identify lateral movement indicators
- Detect beaconing or abnormal session behavior
- Recognize deviations from expected timing and volume patterns

### 5. Correlation & Context
- Correlate network indicators with host and intelligence data
- Support threat attribution and activity assessment
- Provide network context to incident response efforts

### 6. Defensive Cyber Support
- Support detection logic refinement
- Assist with scoping and containment decisions
- Identify potential exfiltration or command-and-control activity

### 7. Reporting & Communication
- Produce network-focused analytical summaries
- Brief findings to analysts, planners, and leadership
- Translate technical observations into mission-relevant risk

---

## Key Attributes
- Pattern recognition
- Systems thinking
- Strong understanding of network fundamentals
- Clear communication of complex behavior

---

## Out of Scope
- Host-based forensic analysis
- Operational planning and MDMP ownership
- Tool or packet-level exploitation techniques
