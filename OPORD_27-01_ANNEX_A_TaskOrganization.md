## A.3 Qualification & Competency Requirements

### Military Positions

**CPT 173 Team Lead (CPT):**
- MOS: 17A (Cyber Operations Officer)
- Security Clearance: Secret (TS/SCI preferred)
- Experience: Minimum 3 years cyber operations planning or incident response
- MDMP Competency: ADP 5-0 and cyber operations process literacy required
- Certifications: Preferred: CCSK (Cloud Security), CEH (Certified Ethical Hacker)

**Deputy Team Lead (WO1/CW2):**
- MOS: 170A (Cyber Warrant Officer)
- Security Clearance: Secret (TS/SCI preferred)
- Experience: Minimum 5 years cyber operations, 2+ years forensics
- Technical Competency: Advanced malware analysis, reverse engineering, incident response
- Certifications: GCIH (GIAC Certified Incident Handler), GREM (GIAC Reverse Engineering Malware)

**Forensics & Host Analysis Lead (SSG/SFC):**
- MOS: 17C (Cyber Operations Technician)
- Security Clearance: Secret
- Experience: Minimum 3 years forensic analysis, endpoint security
- Technical Competency: Memory imaging, artifact analysis, timeline construction, malware behavior analysis
- Certifications: GCFE (GIAC Certified Forensic Examiner), GCIA (GIAC Certified Intrusion Analyst)

**Network Analysis & Hunting Lead (SSG/SFC):**
- MOS: 17C (Cyber Operations Technician)
- Security Clearance: Secret
- Experience: Minimum 3 years network defense, threat hunting, incident response
- Technical Competency: SIEM/EDR tool proficiency, network traffic analysis, hunting hypothesis development
- Certifications: GCIA (GIAC Certified Intrusion Analyst), GPEN (GIAC Penetration Tester)

**Incident Response Lead (SSG/SFC):**
- MOS: 17C (Cyber Operations Technician)
- Security Clearance: Secret
- Experience: Minimum 2 years incident response, containment procedures, playbook execution
- Technical Competency: Incident triage, root cause analysis, containment execution, evidence preservation
- Certifications: GCIH (GIAC Certified Incident Handler), CEH (Certified Ethical Hacker)

**Reporting & Operations NCO (SGT):**
- MOS: 17C (Cyber Operations Technician) or 42A (Human Resources Specialist)
- Security Clearance: Secret
- Experience: Minimum 2 years military operations support, communication skills
- Technical Competency: Case management systems, reporting tools, operations documentation
- Certifications: CompTIA A+, CompTIA Security+

## A.4 Training & Pre-Deployment Preparation

### Pre-Deployment Training (1 Week, Weeks 1-2 MAR 2026)

| Training | Duration | Topic | Responsible |
|----------|----------|-------|-------------|
| **APT43 Threat Brief** | 4 hrs | TTPs, malware, infrastructure, recent activity, attribution | Battalion S2 + NSA liaison |
| **Hawaii Network Architecture** | 2 hrs | DoD networks, HECO systems, SEMA infrastructure, cloud environments | DISA / HECO CSO |
| **HECO/SEMA Coordination** | 2 hrs | Points of contact, escalation procedures, mutual agreements, ROE | HECO/SEMA leadership |
| **Incident Response Playbooks** | 6 hrs | Standard playbooks, containment procedures, escalation thresholds | CPT TL + IR Lead |
| **Tools & Platform Training** | 8 hrs | SIEM (Splunk), EDR (CrowdStrike), network analysis, forensics kit | Battalion IT + tool vendors |
| **Tabletop Exercise** | 4 hrs | Scenario-based incident response (credential compromise, malware, OT threat) | CPT TL + Battalion S3 |
| **Authorities & Legal Briefing** | 2 hrs | DCO-M/DCO-RA authority, ROE, classified information handling, law enforcement liaison | ARCYBER legal officer |
| **OPSEC & Security Procedures** | 2 hrs | Classified material handling, communications security, data protection | NSA security officer |

**Total:** 30 hours over 1 week

### Post-Deployment Orientation (3 Days, 17-19 MAR 2026)

1. **Joint Base Inprocessing:** Security badge, facility access, emergency procedures
2. **MOC Walkthrough:** Secure facility familiarization, equipment location, power/HVAC systems
3. **Network Integration:** Access validation to SIEM, EDR, network detection systems
4. **Initial Team Meeting:** Meet HECO/SEMA security leadership, NSA liaison, FBI contact
5. **System & Sensor Testing:** Validate data flow from all monitored networks

## A.6 Personnel Accountability & Rotation

**Operational Tempo:** 24/7 mission with 6-person element

**Watch Schedule (Proposed):**
- **MOC Watch (2 personnel on duty at all times)**
  - OIC/NCOIC team rotation: 0000-0800Z, 0800-1600Z, 1600-0000Z
  - One analyst minimum on watch at all times

**Off-Duty Rotation:**
- **5-day rotation:** 2 days on-watch (MOC operations) → 1 day off → 2 days on-watch → 2 days off
- Allows team to maintain fitness, attend training, and prevent burnout

**Replacement Planning:**
- Minimum 2 shadow personnel pre-positioned for rotation relief after 60 days
- Quarterly personnel change-out recommended to prevent fatigue and burnout
- One-week changeover period for knowledge transfer
